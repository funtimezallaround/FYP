\section{Introduction to the Area, Motivation for the Project, and a Defense of Why the Problem is Non-Trivial}
The field of sports science has been significantly transformed by the integration of \textbf{Artificial Intelligence (AI)} and \textbf{computer vision (CV)}. These technologies provide objective, scalable methods for performance analysis that overcome the limitations of traditional, subjective coaching and manual data collection \cite{Naik2022}. In competitive swimming, a sport where marginal gains are critical, the precise quantification of a swimmer's technique is a major challenge. Key metrics such as stroke length and absolute speed are essential indicators of a swimmer's efficiency and power, yet they are almost impossible to obtain from standard video without a reliable spatial reference \cite{Dubois2012}.

This project is motivated by the need to democratize access to this valuable kinematic data. Current solutions are often either labor-intensive, relying on human annotators, or prohibitively expensive, requiring specialized multi-camera setups or on-body sensors. For smaller clubs and individual athletes, this creates a significant barrier to data-driven performance enhancement. The problem is non-trivial because it requires a solution that can accurately metricize an environment that is constantly in motion and subject to visual distortions from water and camera angles. Our proposed solution addresses this by identifying and leveraging a ubiquitous, standardized object within the swimming environment: the lanerope.

\section{Aims and Objectives}
The primary aim of this project is to develop and validate a computer vision system that can accurately determine a swimmer's distance traveled and derive key kinematic metrics using the lanerope as a spatial reference. To achieve this, the following objectives have been established:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Develop a computer vision pipeline capable of detecting and tracking the discrete sections of a swimming lanerope from standard video footage.
\item Establish a robust method for calibrating the video's pixel-to-distance ratio based on the known physical dimensions of the lanerope.
\item Integrate the spatial scale with a swimmer tracking algorithm to calculate kinematic metrics, including absolute speed and stroke length.
\item Evaluate the system's accuracy by comparing its calculated distance against known physical markers and quantifying the margin of error.
\item Deliver an objective, low-cost performance analysis tool that can be used by coaches and swimmers with minimal equipment.
\end{enumerate}
